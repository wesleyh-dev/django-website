About Me

My passion for computer science began when I took an introductory CS course during my junior year of college at the suggestion of my roommate. I didn't know what I was getting into at the time, but this course, along with deeply inspiring and passionate professors, sparked a flame that has persisted my entire life. Since then, I earned my Bachelor of Science in Mathematics and Computer Science from Emory University in 2018. With this strong, technical background, I am more than capable of adapting to an ever-expanding technology landscape.

I have worked as an engineer, developer, designer, leader, and even a chef. I love to build things, break things, and learn new things whenever the opportunity presents itself. I love to read about a diverse range of subjects, from applied cryptography to the history of ancient Central American civilizations. I especially love a fresh installation of Linux. For me, it's like taking a breath of fresh morning air.

I have traveled to nearly twenty countries across four continents starting at age 11. My unique exposure to the needs of a diverse range of demographics enables me to effectively assess how a given technology or service can impact the widest audience. It is with this broad range of experiences and knowledge that I am driven to a life serving the public with the aid of innovation, discovery, and, hopefully, bitcoin. My vision of the future is one where everyone has the ability to own their own digital identity and their own digital bank vault, securely.
